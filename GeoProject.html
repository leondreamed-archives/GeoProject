Sambaa K'e National Park

Welcome to the Park of the Taiga Plains

Located in the NT part of the Mid-Boreal Taiga Plains Ecozone, near Trout Lake.

Geographical Features:

Landform Description: Our park is situated upon a rolling landscape. Poorly drained till plains formed wetlands in our area, providing visitors with a unique experience.

Climate: Visitors coming should expect a warm and moist climate in the summer, but a very cold and snowy winter. Overall, temperatures average -2℃ annually. There is little precipitation (400 - 460mm), and permafrost occurs about 60cm deep in the soil. In the winter, snow and ice last 6 - 8 months before melting, resulting in a relatively short summer.

Vegetation Type: Mixed-wood, deciduous, and coniferous forests are scattered throughout the area. The understory vegetation contains a diverse range of plants.

Soil Type: The main types of soil found in our area include Bruinsols, Luvisols, Cryosols and Organic Soils.
